Detailed Description

These routines form the heart of our geometry free numerical algorithms. They are called by our geometric operators like the arakawa bracket.

Push forward a vector from cylindrical to a new coordinate system.

Computes

\[ v^x(x,y) = x_R (x,y) v^R(R(x,y), Z(x,y)) + x_Z v^Z(R(x,y), Z(x,y)) \\ v^y(x,y) = y_R (x,y) v^R(R(x,y), Z(x,y)) + y_Z v^Z(R(x,y), Z(x,y)) \]

where \( x_R = \frac{\partial x}{\partial R}\), ...

Computes \( v^i = g^{ij}v_j\) for \( i,j\in \{1,2\}\) in the two dimensions of a 2x1 product space
